4. A and B start a business, with A investing the total capital of Rs.50000, on the condition that B pays A 
interest @ 10% per annum on his half of the capital. A is a working partner and receives Rs.1500 per  
 
month from the total profit and any profit remaining is equally shared by both of them. At the end of 
the year, it was found that the income of A is twice that of B. Find the total profit for the year? 
 
A. 53000  
B. 58000  
C. 50000  
D. 59000 
 
Answer: Option D 
 
Solution: 
 
Interest received by A from B = 10% of half of Rs.50000 = 10% * 25000 = 2500. 
Amount received by A per annum for being a working partner = 1500 * 12 = Rs.18000. 
Let 'P' be the part of the remaining profit that A receives as his share. Total income of A = (2500 + 18000 
+ P) 
Total income of B = only his share from the remaining profit = 'P', as A and B share the remaining profit 
equally. 
Income of A = Twice the income of B 
(2500 + 18000 + P) = 2(P) 
P = 20500 
Total profit = 2P + 18000 
= 2*20500 + 18000 = 59000
