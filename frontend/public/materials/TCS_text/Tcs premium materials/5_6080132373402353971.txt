wrong(S1).  
If there are 2 letters and 2 envelopes then you can put them wrong in 1 
way(S2). If there are 3 letters and 3 envelopes then you can put them wrong in 
2 ways(S3). If there are 4 then you can put them wrong in 9 ways(S4). 
If there are 5 then you can put them wrong in 44 ways(S5). 
If you observe you can find a pattern. 
S3=(S1+S2)*2 
S4=(S2+S3)*3 
S5=(S3+S4)*4 
S6=(S4+S5)*5 
In general, Sn=(Sn-2 + Sn-1)*(n-1) 
So, if there are 5 letters then S5=(S3+S4)*4=(2+9)*4=44

When dividing our number nn by 4 we obtain a remainder of 1, so, if the

thus we can write Q=5q+4.Q=5q+4.  
It follows that n=4(5q+4)+1=20q+17.n=4(5q+4)+1=20q+17.
